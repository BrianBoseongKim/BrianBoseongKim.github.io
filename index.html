I am a robotics autonomy engineer specializing in drone-based exploration and real-world autonomy, with a strong focus on perception, state estimation, and decision-making under uncertainty. My work lies at the intersection of Physical AI and large-scale robotic systems, where algorithms must operate reliably beyond controlled environments.

During my Ph.D. at KAIST, I led autonomous UAV systems for complex and unstructured environments, including underground exploration, multi-floor inspection, and search operations. I served as the UAV Team Lead in the DARPA Subterranean Challenge Final Event in collaboration with NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) as part of Team CoSTAR, where we tackled large-scale autonomy in GPS-denied environments.

My experience spans from research to real-world deployment, including autonomous indoor drone systems, LiDAR-inertial odometry, and scalable SLAM for industrial environments. At Samsung Electronics and Field AI, I have worked on large-scale perception systems, digital twins, and risk-aware autonomy for complex robotic platforms.

I have led multiple award-winning teams in national and international competitions, including 1st place in the AI Grand Challenge (Autonomous UAV), 1st place in the Hyundai Autonomous Driving Challenge, and top rankings in DARPA SubT and Indy Autonomous Challenge. My research has been recognized with multiple Best Paper Awards in robotics conferences.

I am particularly interested in pushing the boundary of scalable autonomy—building systems that can perceive, explore, and make decisions in previously unseen environments, bringing us closer to truly autonomous robotic agents operating in the real world.
